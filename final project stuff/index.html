Trump's Plan for Reevaluation

(Background)

Since taking public office, the Trump administration has wasted no time in making major changes to decades-old foreign and domestic policy, one of which included halting all foreign aid until further notice. This project aims to assess the necessity and implications of this decision. The United States has historically been one of the largest contributors to foreign aid globally, providing financial assistance for humanitarian efforts, economic development, and geopolitical stability.

By analyzing the U.S. government’s publicly available foreign income dataset, this research will explore historical trends in foreign aid allocation across different regions, funding groups, and time periods. Foreign aid plays a crucial role in diplomacy, disaster relief, and economic growth in developing nations. However, recent political shifts have raised questions about its sustainability and strategic importance.

This study will also examine how U.S. foreign aid spending compares to other government expenditures, evaluating whether the recent cuts were financially justified or politically motivated. Through visual data analysis, we aim to present a comprehensive picture of how aid reductions impact recipient countries and international relations.

What aid are we administering?

(Source)

The dataset for this project is published by the U.S. Office of Foreign Assistance and provides comprehensive data on U.S. foreign aid allocations, including recipient countries, managing agencies, funding accounts, aid objectives, and transaction types. The most recent update to the dataset was in December 2024, ensuring that our analysis is based on the latest available data.

The U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID), which manages much of the country’s foreign aid, plays a central role in global development efforts. Established in 1961, USAID’s mission is to promote international stability, reduce poverty, and advance humanitarian assistance. It oversees aid distribution to support economic growth, democracy, and disaster relief initiatives worldwide.

The dataset tracks financial commitments and disbursements over multiple years, allowing for a detailed analysis of historical trends in U.S. foreign assistance. It includes information on recipient countries, aid categories, and project types, offering insights into how aid priorities have shifted over time. This study will assess how the U.S. government's role in global development has evolved, particularly in light of recent policy changes.
